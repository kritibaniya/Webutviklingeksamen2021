Blood donation

We have taken our starting point in the sustainable development goal 3: good health and well-being. The goal is to seek good health and well-being for all, at every stage of life, and it addresses all major health priorities. Within this, lack of blood is a major problem. We are always in need of blood doners, to save lives and to improve it. Read more about how important this problem is here and get a deeper and broader insight. Our solution is based on this, where we focus on facilitating this.


Technological solution

We have developed an app which will help us accomplish our goal. Which is to be able to create a platform that will connect those who can give with people in need. It is a user-friendly app with few, but very important and useful functionalities. Since we aim to target people from all over the world, we want it to be easier for targeted users to utilize.


A usecase which will give you an insight on how our app works:

Scenario: Superman wants to be a blood done
Actor: Superman
Pre-condition: Superman has pre downloaded our app

  • Step 1:He will need to create his profile by registering his email and phone number.
  • Step 2: He can either book an appointment for health checkup or get information about nearest health post through the app and contact them directly.
  • Step 3: Superman needs to meet up at the health station for his blood test and health checkup.
  • Step 4: Only if Superman is healthy and capable of being a blood doner, will an authorized health care worker fill his health data and verify his user. Superman is now verified and ready to save lives!
  • Step 5: Sammy waits for notification and a text message every time there is an emergency need of blood in his area.

Our future

Blood donation is our first step toward accomplishing UN’s sustainable goal. We hope that in future, we can go further ahead and be able to gather organ doners as well. Many people are born with organ failure, or have their organs damaged due to injuries. Organ transplantation is a modern medicine, which have saved plenty of lives. However, there are hundreds of people who don’t have access to doners and potential doners who don’t have much information regarding this issue.
