Problem

Why is it important with blood donations, and why are we short?

In today's society lack of blood is a problem, and in most cases, more blood is sent out then what has been collected. Donations are always required, since blood transfusion helps save lives and improve health. To elaborate, it is an important part in the completion of several operations, this can e.g. be for organ, marrow, and cancer surgeries. Furthermore, there are patients who need blood transfusions regularly throughout their lives. According to the American Red Cross [Source1], 36,000 units of red blood cells, 7,000 units of platelets and 10,000 units of plasma are needed daily in the U.S.

Furthermore, in developing countries, blood donations is a big issue. If we take Africa as an example, then blood is a rare commodity in large parts of the continent. both because there is a lack of public awareness, and a lack of cold storage facilities. This makes it difficult to keep and distribute the blood they have already stored. The rural clinics are more affected by this compared to urban hospitals, due to restrictions on blood supply.

There has been done a research [Source2] from a consortium of American hematologists, that has revealed the gap between blood supply and demand in several developing countries. The study compares blood availability estimates compiled by the World Health Organization. Several researchers have said that this research is the most detailed analysis ever published of the world's blood needs-to-supply ratio. The result from the research stated, that of the 180 countries analyzed, 107 of the countries had no blood to meet their needs.
